Exposure triangle calculator
Interactive aperture, shutter, and ISO controls that show equivalent exposure changes and the practical tradeoffs of each choice.

Equivalent exposure wheel
A rotating visual dial that lets students swap one setting for another while maintaining the same overall exposure.
Motion blur and shutter speed simulator

A slider that shows how people, cars, water, and camera shake render at different shutter speeds.
Depth of field visualizer

A tool that changes aperture, focal length, subject distance, and sensor size so students can see near and far focus zones expand or collapse.
Focal length and angle-of-view comparer

Side-by-side frames showing how 24mm, 35mm, 50mm, 85mm, 135mm, etc. change framing from the same camera position.
Perspective vs focal length demonstrator

A lesson showing that camera position changes perspective, while focal length mainly changes framing and field of view.
Handholding safety chart

An interactive guide for minimum handheld shutter speeds based on focal length, stabilization, and subject movement.
Metering mode simulator

A scene-based tool showing how evaluative, center-weighted, and spot metering behave in high-contrast conditions.
Exposure compensation trainer

A set of tricky scenes like snow, backlight, dark clothing, stage light where students decide when to add or subtract exposure.
Histogram interpreter

A visual lesson that connects actual images to histogram shapes so students learn clipping, midtone placement, and dynamic range.
Highlight and shadow clipping lab

A before/after tool showing what detail is lost in blown highlights and blocked shadows, and when that loss matters.
White balance and color temperature slider

A visual comparison tool that shifts a scene through tungsten, daylight, shade, fluorescent, and custom Kelvin values.
Reciprocity and reciprocity failure calculator

A film-focused tool that starts with metered exposure and shows corrected long-exposure values for specific films.
Push/pull film decision chart

An interactive guide showing what happens when film is exposed or developed differently from box speed, including contrast and shadow effects.
Framing cleanup exercise

Students drag crop boundaries or reposition a frame to reduce clutter, tangencies, mergers, and dead space.
Basic composition guide

A clean visual tool covering horizon placement, subject placement, balance, negative space, and foreground-background relationships.
Advanced composition variation guide

A more sophisticated lesson showing edge tension, visual weight, layering, repetition, interruption, geometric alignment, and figure-ground separation.
Lens choice by scenario selector

Students choose a lens for portraits, interiors, street, sports, landscapes, and documentary situations and see the consequences of each choice.
Focus mode and autofocus area trainer

A scenario-based tool for single AF, continuous AF, manual focus, face detect, zone focus, and tracking behavior.
“What went wrong?” diagnostic lab

A troubleshooting tool where students inspect failed images and identify missed focus, camera shake, bad exposure, poor framing, subject merge, or wrong timing.
